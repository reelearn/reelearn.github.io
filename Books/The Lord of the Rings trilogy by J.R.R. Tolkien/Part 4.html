The Lord of the Rings trilogy by J.R.R. Tolkien

Part 4: The Shire and the Hobbits

The Shire is a peaceful and idyllic place where the hobbits live. It is the home of Frodo and the other main hobbit characters. The Shire represents a simple and contented way of life, untouched by the conflicts and darkness of the outside world. However, throughout the story, the Shire is threatened by the spreading influence of evil. When Frodo and his companions return to the Shire after their long journey, they find it under the control of Saruman, a former ally turned enemy. The hobbits, led by Frodo and Sam, rise up against the invaders and restore peace to their beloved Shire. This part of the story emphasizes the resilience and bravery of the hobbits, who despite their small stature, are able to stand up against great adversity.
