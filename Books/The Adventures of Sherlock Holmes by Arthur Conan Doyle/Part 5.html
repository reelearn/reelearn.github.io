The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes by Arthur Conan Doyle

Part 5: The Final Problem

In 'The Final Problem,' Holmes' arch-nemesis, Professor Moriarty, poses a significant threat to society. Moriarty is a criminal mastermind who controls a vast criminal network. Holmes realizes that he must eliminate Moriarty in order to protect innocent lives. The story follows Holmes and Watson as they flee from Moriarty's henchmen and engage in a deadly game of wits. In the end, Holmes and Moriarty face each other at the Reichenbach Falls, where they both fall to their apparent deaths. This story marks the supposed end of Holmes' career as a detective and showcases his willingness to sacrifice himself for the greater good.
