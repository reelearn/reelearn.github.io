The Little Prince by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

Part 3: The Snake and the Desert

As the Little Prince continues his journey, he encounters a snake in the desert. The snake represents death and the transient nature of life. The snake offers to help the Little Prince return to his planet, but the Little Prince refuses, determined to find his own way back. This encounter highlights the Little Prince's courage and his desire to take control of his own destiny. It also emphasizes the inevitability of death and the importance of living each moment to the fullest.
