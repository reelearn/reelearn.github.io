Harry Potter series by J.K. Rowling

Part 1: The Boy Who Lived

Harry Potter, a young orphan, lives with his abusive relatives, the Dursleys. On his eleventh birthday, he discovers that he is a wizard and receives an invitation to attend Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Harry learns about his parents' tragic death and his connection to the dark wizard, Lord Voldemort. He also discovers that he is famous in the wizarding world for surviving an attack from Voldemort as a baby. With the help of Hagrid, the gamekeeper at Hogwarts, Harry enters the magical world and begins his journey as a wizard.

Part 2: The Philosopher's Stone

At Hogwarts, Harry makes friends with Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger. Together, they uncover a plot to steal the Philosopher's Stone, an object that grants immortality. Harry, Ron, and Hermione use their skills and bravery to overcome various obstacles, including a deadly chess game and a three-headed dog. They ultimately defeat the dark wizard who seeks the stone and save Hogwarts from destruction.

Part 3: The Chamber of Secrets

In Harry's second year at Hogwarts, a mysterious monster called the Basilisk petrifies students. Harry discovers the Chamber of Secrets, where a young Voldemort had hidden a deadly creature. With the help of his friends, Harry uncovers the truth and defeats the monster. He also learns more about his connection to Voldemort and the importance of loyalty and friendship.

Part 4: The Prisoner of Azkaban

A dangerous criminal, Sirius Black, escapes from the wizarding prison, Azkaban. Harry learns that Sirius was his parents' best friend and betrayed them to Voldemort. As Harry investigates the truth, he discovers the existence of time travel and the importance of confronting one's fears. With the help of his friends and a surprising twist, Harry saves Sirius and learns the power of love and forgiveness.

Part 5: The Goblet of Fire

Hogwarts hosts the Triwizard Tournament, a dangerous competition between three wizarding schools. Despite being underage, Harry is mysteriously chosen as a participant. Throughout the tournament, Harry faces numerous challenges and dangers, including battling a dragon and diving into a lake filled with mermaids. In the end, Harry discovers that the tournament was a trap set by Voldemort to regain his power. Harry barely escapes with his life, but Cedric Diggory, a fellow competitor, tragically dies.

Part 6: The Order of the Phoenix

Harry returns to Hogwarts for his fifth year, only to find the wizarding world in denial about Voldemort's return. He forms a secret group called Dumbledore's Army to teach his fellow students defensive magic. Harry faces numerous challenges, including encountering visions of Voldemort's actions and battling Death Eaters. He also learns about the prophecy that links him to Voldemort and the importance of unity in the fight against darkness. The book ends with the tragic death of Harry's beloved mentor, Sirius Black.
