The Diary of a Young Girl by Anne Frank

Part 2: Relationships and Conflicts

In this part, Anne delves into the relationships and conflicts within the secret annex. She writes about her complicated relationship with her sister, Margot, and her growing affection for Peter, the son of another family in hiding. Anne also describes the tensions that arise from living in close quarters with different personalities and the challenges of maintaining harmony. Through her diary, Anne provides a unique perspective on the dynamics of human relationships under extreme circumstances.
