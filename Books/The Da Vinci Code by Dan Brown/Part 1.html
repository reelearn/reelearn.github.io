The Da Vinci Code by Dan Brown

Part 1: The Murder at the Louvre

The Da Vinci Code starts with the shocking murder of Jacques Saunière, the curator of the Louvre Museum in Paris. Before dying, Saunière leaves behind a series of cryptic clues and symbols, including a message written in his own blood. The police call in Robert Langdon, a renowned symbologist, to decipher the mysterious message. Langdon teams up with Sophie Neveu, a cryptologist and Saunière's granddaughter, to unravel the truth behind the murder. As they delve deeper into the investigation, they discover a secret society, the Priory of Sion, and a powerful religious organization, Opus Dei, who are both desperate to obtain a hidden secret that could shake the foundations of Christianity.
