And Then There Were None by Agatha Christie

Part 4: The Second Death

The tension escalates as the second death takes place. Mrs. Rogers, the housekeeper, is found dead in her sleep. The guests become even more suspicious of each other, wondering who among them is the killer. Paranoia grips their minds, and they start questioning every action and motive. With each death, they realize that the killer is among them, playing a deadly game of cat and mouse. As they struggle to stay alive, they must also confront their own guilt and face the consequences of their past actions.
